# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Event coordinators, banquet managers, F&B directors, and service staff at hotels and venues. They operate under time pressure in varied environments — bright kitchens, dim ballrooms, and active service floors — often on tablets while moving through event spaces. Their job is to coordinate guest lists, seating, menu planning, kitchen prep, service timing, and staff assignment so nothing falls through the cracks.

## Product Purpose

A single operational hub that replaces fragmented spreadsheets, paper checklists, and walkie-talkie chaos for party planning and kitchen/service management. Success means an event coordinator can open the app at any moment and know exactly what needs attention, what's on track, and who's responsible — without hunting across tools or calling around.

## Brand Personality

Composed, precise, dependable. The calm in the storm — a tool that stays reliable when everything around it is hectic.

## Anti-references

- Consumer-party aesthetic (balloons, confetti, neon brights)
- Social/guest-facing RSVP tools disguised as professional software
- Dashboards overloaded with metrics nobody reads
- Generic SaaS templates with stock illustrations
- Bloated enterprise tools that require training to complete basic tasks

## Design Principles

1. **Speed under pressure** — every action reachable in minimal taps or clicks
2. **Progressive disclosure** — the right detail at the right moment, not everything at once
3. **Trust through clarity** — status immediately scannable, never ambiguous
4. **Offline-first respect** — network is spotty in basements and kitchens; design for resilience
5. **One source of truth** — no duplicated or conflicting information across views

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA target
- High contrast modes for varying ambient light (bright kitchens to dim ballrooms)
- Touch targets sized for hurried or gloved fingers
- Reduced motion respected across all animations
